Team 5 · Trapped Ions

The Qubitarium
of Trapped Ions

A theatrical performance in six acts

Simon is your narrator and stage director. Dot, Bob, and Sneaky are your ions. The audience provides the control fields, the photons, and the measurement.

I Vacuum & Emergence

Simon welcomes the audience to the Qubitarium — a vacuum where three ions are suspended in layered electromagnetic fields.

Simon says: "Close your eyes."

In darkness, the three ions move onto the stage. Each stands inside their own circular "layer," representing their position within the trap.

  • Three distinct ions, each suspended within their own invisible field.
  • No chaos. Just presence.

The vacuum chamber is established. Ions are confined but not yet initialized.

II Electromagnetic Rotation

The ions oscillate subtly between two orientations.

Simon says: "Left side of the room — sing C. Right side — sing G."

"These are your two basis states."

The sustained harmonics create a control field. The ions begin rotating in place — shifting posture between two alignments.

  • They are not choosing.
  • They are resonating between two control fields.

Control pulses rotate the qubit state.

III Optical Pumping

The ions are still moving slightly.

Simon says: "Turn on your phone flashlights and point them at the ions."

The audience illuminates the stage — each phone a photon source.

Gradually, the ions stop jumping. They become still. Initialized into a known ground state.

  • Stabilized — but not yet entangled.

Photon interaction optically pumps the ions into their ground state.

IV Rotation Pulse

Simon says: "Imagine you are still singing C and G."

Table 5 sharply raises their phone lights in unison — a sudden, visible pulse.

Simon says: "Tilt your phones right…"

Simon says: "Tilt your phones left."

Two ions begin a slow, controlled circular walk toward each other.

A pulse drives coherent rotation on the Bloch sphere.

V Entanglement

Two ions pair and begin synchronized circles — ring around the rosy.

Their movements mirror each other precisely. If one shifts, the other shifts. They are now coupled.

Entangled qubits sharing a joint quantum state.

VI Measurement

An audience participant picks a state out of a hat.

They walk between the entangled ions and announce their choice aloud: "C" or "G."

The ions release hands and form a C or G with their arms — YMCA style.

  • Measurement complete.
  • State resolved.
  • No more blending.

Measurement collapses the qubit into one eigenstate.
